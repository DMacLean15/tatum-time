🏀 Writing a Great Tatum Time Post

Everything's optional — plain writing already looks great. Use the preview on the right to see how it'll look.

⭐ Make it pop

  • Give it a punchy headline — it's the first thing readers see.
  • Keep paragraphs short. Break up long posts with headings.
  • Add a photo or YouTube clip — posts with visuals get read more.
  • Sprinkle in green stat tags for the key numbers.
  • Emojis work anywhere in your writing, not just the banner. 🍀🔥
  • Blank page? Start from a 📋 Post Recipe instead (see below).

🚀 One-click helpers

  • 📋 Post Recipes (button, bottom-right) — ready-made post structures (Game Recap, Trade Reaction, Player Spotlight, Power Rankings, Hot Take). Hit Copy, paste into the Article, and fill in the blanks. The same panel lists the current roster — click a name to copy it (spelled right, it auto-links to the player's card).
  • 📋 Copy last game as a Game Score — on the Celtics scoreboard (bottom-left), turns the real last game into a score card you can paste straight into a recap.
  • 🌐 View Live Site (button, bottom-right) — opens the published site in a new tab to check your work.
  • Recipes and blocks paste cleanest in the editor's Markdown view (the toggle at the top of the writing box). Switch back to normal view anytime.

📝 The fields at the top — what each one does

  • Headline — the post's title.
  • Banner Emoji — one emoji shown on the post's card (🏀 🍀 🔥 🏆 🎯).
  • Hero Photo — the big photo at the top of the post (details below). Optional.
  • Hero Photo Focus — which part of that photo to keep when it crops to a banner (Center / Top / Bottom / Left / Right).
  • Category — pick one: Recap, Analysis, News, Playoffs, Draft, Roster, or Milestone. It shows as a tag and powers the category filter buttons.
  • ⭐ Feature this post — turn ON to pin it to the big "Featured Post" spot on the homepage. Leave OFF and your newest post is featured automatically. Only turn it on for one post.
  • Date — pick from the calendar (new posts default to today). This is what orders your posts — no dragging needed.
  • Game Box-Score Link — adds a "See full game stats" button (see below).
  • Article — the post body, where you write.

📋 Post starters (the structures behind the Recipes button)

🏀 Game recap: Final score → 1–2 standout performers (with stat tags) → the turning point → what's next.

📰 Trade / news reaction: What happened → why it matters for the C's → your grade or hot take.

⭐ Player spotlight: Quick intro → the numbers that pop → why he matters to the team.

🟢 Green stat tags

Wrap any stat in == to make a green highlight:

Tatum went off for ==28.3 PPG== → 28.3 PPG on the live site.

Heads up: in the preview it shows as plain ==28.3 PPG==, but it turns green on the published site.

✍️ Formatting toolbar

Use the toolbar above the writing box for bold, italics, Headings, bullet/numbered lists, links, and quotes. Use Headings to break a longer post into clear sections.

  • Add a link: select some words, click the link button, and paste any web address — a news article, an NBA page, or even one of your own Tatum Time posts.
  • Quote button (") — makes a short quote with a green bar, great for quoting a player or reporter. For a big emphasis quote, use the Pull Quote block instead.
  • Prefer typing? You can type markdown directly: ## Heading, **bold**, - list item, > quote.
Example

Typing markdown works too: **bold** → bold,  *italics* → italics,  ## Heading → a section heading.

📸 Photos

  • Click the image button and upload — it auto-resizes to fit.
  • Type a description when you add a photo and it shows as a small caption underneath it on the site.
  • The little thumbnail sometimes doesn't show while attaching — that's cosmetic. Check the preview to confirm the photo's there.
  • Reuse a photo: the image button remembers past uploads — pick one from the list instead of uploading it again.
  • You can add several photos in one post.
  • Shrink huge phone photos before uploading so the site stays fast.

🖼️ Hero Photo

Set a Hero Photo (field near the top) and it shows big at the top of the post and on the post cards. Leave it blank and the first photo in your article is used automatically — so old posts already look great.

The top photo crops to a wide banner. If it cuts off the important part, use Hero Photo Focus to pick which part to keep in view (e.g. Top to keep a face). Most photos look best on Center.

🏀 "See full game stats" button

Fill in the Game Box-Score Link field with an NBA.com box-score web address, and a green button appears on the post. Leave it blank for none.

It should look like: https://www.nba.com/game/lal-vs-bos-0022500123/box-score

💬 Reactions & comments (automatic — you add nothing)

Every post gets emoji reactions and a comment section on its own. You'll get an email whenever someone comments on a post or subscribes to the newsletter.

🍀 Publishing & ordering

  • Posts sort themselves by Date, newest first — no dragging. To reorder, just change a post's Date field.
  • Use ⭐ Feature this post to pin one post to the homepage's Featured spot.
  • Each post is its own entry — use the search box in the post list to find one fast.
  • Edit or delete a post: open it from the list — Delete entry is at the top of the editor. Your change goes live after you hit Publish.
  • Get it looking right in the preview, then hit Publish once. Edit as much as you want afterward — it's free and unlimited.
