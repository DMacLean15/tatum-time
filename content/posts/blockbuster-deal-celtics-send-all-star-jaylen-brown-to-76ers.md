The Celtics shipped their All-Star Jaylen Brown (Ranked 4th in PPG in the NBA) to the 76ers for Veteran Paul George and 4 draft picks.

They addressed their need to fill the gap at SF by obtaining PG-13 and gained some potentially nice future picks but at what cost? Why didn't they just go all in on Giannis Antetokounmpo?

![](/images/uploads/img_0158.webp)

![](/images/uploads/screenshot-2026-07-02-at-04-31-16-nba-futures-2026-27-nba-finals-champion-odds-espn.png)

Somehow, they still manage to have the 4th best odds to win next season's NBA Championship.

Paul George's injury history and age (36) scare me. He’s only appeared in 70+ games twice since the 2018-2019 season:

![](/images/uploads/screenshot-2026-07-02-at-05-33-09-paul-george-career-stats-nba-espn.png)

The flip side of it is, he is a well-balanced veteran that can bring a lot to the squad \*IF\* he can play ~50-60 games and that's a big what if at this point.

Obviously, Brad and the men upstairs know what they are doing but only time will tell if this one works out.
