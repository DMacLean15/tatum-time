================================================================
   HEY DAN — LET'S GET TATUM TIME ONLINE  ☘️
   A no-experience-needed guide. Just follow it top to bottom.
================================================================

Don't worry — you don't need to know ANY code. You'll be
clicking buttons and typing your email a few times. Total time
is about 30–40 minutes, and you only EVER do this once. After
that, posting a new blog is just filling out a form.

You'll make two free accounts:
   • GitHub  — a place that stores your website's files
   • Netlify — puts the site on the internet + handles your login

Keep this guide open in one window and do the steps in another.
If you get stuck on any step, take a screenshot and send it to
whoever gave you this — they can unstick you in seconds.


================================================================
  PART 0 — GET YOUR WEBSITE FILES READY  (2 min)
================================================================
1. You were sent a file called  "TatumTime-Website.zip".
   Save it somewhere easy, like your Desktop.
2. Right-click it  ->  "Extract All..."  ->  "Extract"
   (on a Mac: just double-click it).
3. You now have a FOLDER called "TatumTime-Website" (or
   "tatum-time") full of files. Leave that folder open — you'll
   grab files from it in Part 2.

   ✅ You're ready.


================================================================
  PART 1 — MAKE A GITHUB ACCOUNT  (5 min)
================================================================
1. Open a web browser and go to:   github.com
2. Click "Sign up".
3. Type your email, make up a password, and pick a username
   (anything is fine, like  dmaclean15 ).
4. It may ask you to solve a little picture puzzle to prove
   you're human — do that.
5. It emails you a code. Open your email, copy the code, paste
   it in.
6. If it asks questions like "how many team members" or shows
   paid plans, choose the FREE option / "just me" / skip.

   ✅ You now have GitHub. Leave this tab open and signed in.


================================================================
  PART 2 — PUT YOUR WEBSITE FILES ON GITHUB  (8 min)
================================================================
1. While signed in to GitHub, click the "+" sign in the
   TOP-RIGHT corner of the page  ->  "New repository".
   ("Repository" is just GitHub's word for a project folder.)
2. In "Repository name", type:   tatum-time
3. Make sure the circle next to "Public" is selected.
4. Click the green "Create repository" button at the bottom.
5. The next page has some text in the middle. Find and click the
   blue link that says  "uploading an existing file".
6. Now go to your "TatumTime-Website" folder from Part 0.
   - Click on one file inside it, then press Ctrl+A (Cmd+A on
     Mac) to select EVERYTHING in the folder.
   - Drag all of it onto the big upload box in your browser.
   - It's important the smaller folders named "content", "admin"
     and "images" come along — they will, just drag everything.
7. Wait until all the file names finish appearing in the list.
8. Click the green "Commit changes" button (bottom of page).

   ✅ Your website's files now live on GitHub. Nice work.


================================================================
  PART 3 — MAKE A NETLIFY ACCOUNT + PUT THE SITE LIVE  (8 min)
================================================================
1. Open a new tab and go to:   netlify.com
2. Click "Sign up".
3. Choose the button "Sign up with GitHub" (this links the two
   accounts so you don't need another password). Click
   "Authorize" if it asks.
4. Once you're on the Netlify dashboard, click
   "Add new site"  ->  "Import an existing project".
5. Click "Deploy with GitHub" (click "Authorize"/"Configure" if
   it asks, and allow access to your repositories).
6. In the list, click your project:  tatum-time
7. DON'T type anything in the boxes on the next screen. Just
   scroll down and click the "Deploy" button.
8. Wait about 30 seconds. You'll see a web address near the top
   that looks like   https://something-random-123.netlify.app
   Click it — YOUR SITE IS LIVE ON THE INTERNET. 🎉☘️

   (Optional, to get a nicer address)
9. Click "Site configuration" -> "Change site name", type
   something like  tatumtime , and save. Your address becomes
   https://tatumtime.netlify.app  — write your address down!


================================================================
  PART 4 — TURN ON YOUR LOGIN  (6 min)
================================================================
This is what lets YOU (and only you) sign in to write posts.

1. In your site's menu on the left, look for the word
   "Identity". (If you can't find it, use the search box at the
   top of Netlify and type "Identity".) Click it.
2. Click the "Enable Identity" button.
3. On that same Identity page, find "Registration preferences"
   (or just "Registration") and set it to  "Invite only".
4. Now find a section called "Services". Under it, click
   "Enable Git Gateway".

   ✅ The hard part is officially over.


================================================================
  PART 5 — CREATE YOUR WRITER LOGIN  (3 min)
================================================================
1. Still on the Identity page, click "Invite users".
2. Type YOUR OWN email address and send the invite.
3. Check your email for a message from your site. Click the
   link inside it.
4. It opens your website with a box asking you to set a
   password. Pick a password — this is your writer login.

   ✅ You're now the official author of Tatum Time.


================================================================
  PART 6 — WRITE YOUR FIRST BLOG POST  🏀
================================================================
1. Go to your website address with  /admin  added on the end,
   for example:   https://tatumtime.netlify.app/admin
2. Log in with your email and the password you just made.
3. Click "Blog Posts"  ->  "All Posts".
4. Click "Add Posts" to start a new one. Fill in the boxes:
      • Headline       — your title
      • Banner Emoji   — one emoji, like 🏀 or 🍀
      • Category       — pick one from the little menu
      • Date           — type today's date
      • Game Box-Score Link — OPTIONAL. If your post is about one
        game, go to NBA.com, open that game's box score, copy
        the web address, and paste it here. A green "See full
        game stats" button shows up by itself. Leave blank if
        you don't want it.
      • Article        — write your post! The little toolbar does
        bold, headings and lists. For photos, videos, and green
        stat tags, see "YOUR STYLING CHEAT SHEET" just below.
5. IMPORTANT: drag your new post to the TOP of the list so it
   shows up as the newest.
6. Click the "Publish" button at the top.
7. Wait about a minute, then open your website and refresh.
   Your post is live for the world. You did it! 🎉


================================================================
  YOUR STYLING CHEAT SHEET  —  how a post gets dressed up
================================================================
All of this is OPTIONAL. Plain writing already looks great. But
here are the little tricks and a couple of things to know.

📝 PREVIEW AS YOU TYPE
   While you're in the "Article" box, a live PREVIEW appears on the
   RIGHT (on a small screen there's a button to show/hide it). It's
   styled to look like the real blog, so you can see how your post
   will look before you publish. Your newest post is at the TOP of
   the preview.

✍️ BASIC FORMATTING
   Use the little toolbar above the writing box for BOLD, italics,
   Headings (great for section titles), bullet lists, and links.

🟢 GREEN STAT TAGS
   Put == around any stat to turn it into a green highlight tag:
        Tatum went off for ==28.3 PPG== last month
   ONE thing to know: in the PREVIEW this shows as plain "==28.3
   PPG==", but on the LIVE site it turns green. That's the only
   thing the preview can't show you — everything else is accurate.

📸 PHOTOS
   Click the image button in the toolbar and upload a photo. It
   resizes itself to fit the post automatically — nothing else to do.
   • Sometimes the little thumbnail doesn't show while you're
     attaching it. That's just a cosmetic hiccup — the photo IS
     there. Glance at the PREVIEW on the right to confirm it.
   • Tip: if a photo is huge (a raw phone photo can be 5+ MB),
     shrink it a little before uploading so the site stays fast
     for readers.

▶️ YOUTUBE VIDEOS
   Paste a YouTube link on its OWN line and it becomes a playable
   video right in the post. Just the plain link works, e.g.:
        https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XXXXXXXXXXX

🏀 GAME STATS BUTTON
   Fill in the "Game Box-Score Link" field (an NBA.com box-score
   web address) and a green "See full game stats" button appears
   on the post. Leave it blank for no button.

🍀 BANNER EMOJI
   The "Banner Emoji" you pick shows on the post's banner card.

When it looks right in the preview, drag the post to the TOP of the
list and hit Publish. That's it!
