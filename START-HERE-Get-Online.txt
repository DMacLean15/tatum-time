================================================================
  TATUM TIME — GET THE SITE ONLINE + LET DAN POST (FULL GUIDE)
  Written for someone who has never done this before.
  Take it one step at a time. It takes about 30–40 minutes, ONCE.
================================================================

WHAT YOU'RE BUILDING
--------------------
1) The website, live on the internet at its own address.
2) A private "write a post" page where Dan logs in and publishes
   new blog posts by filling out a simple form — no code, ever.

You need TWO free accounts. Both are 100% free for this:
   • GitHub  — stores the website's files
   • Netlify — puts the site online and handles Dan's login

WHY GITHUB? The posting tool saves new posts by tucking them
into the files on GitHub. That's what makes "no code" possible.
You set this up once; after that you never touch GitHub again.

Have the "tatum-time" folder handy (the one with index.html in
it). You'll upload it in Part 2.


================================================================
  PART 1 — CREATE A GITHUB ACCOUNT  (about 5 min)
================================================================
1. Go to   https://github.com
2. Click "Sign up". Enter an email, pick a password and a
   username (anything, e.g. tatumtime-dan).
3. Verify the email if it asks (check your inbox, click the link).
That's it. Leave this tab open.


================================================================
  PART 2 — UPLOAD THE WEBSITE FILES TO GITHUB  (about 8 min)
================================================================
1. Once signed in, click the "+" in the top-right corner of
   GitHub  ->  "New repository".
2. "Repository name": type   tatum-time
3. Make sure "Public" is selected.
4. Click the green "Create repository" button.
5. On the next page, find and click the link that says
   "uploading an existing file"
   (it's in a line of text near the middle of the page).
6. Open your "tatum-time" folder on your computer. Select
   EVERYTHING inside it (click one file, then Ctrl+A to select
   all), and DRAG it onto the GitHub upload box.
   - Make sure the "content" and "admin" folders come along.
     GitHub keeps the folders for you.
7. Wait for the files to finish listing, then click the green
   "Commit changes" button at the bottom.

Your website's files now live on GitHub.


================================================================
  PART 3 — CREATE A NETLIFY ACCOUNT + PUT THE SITE ONLINE  (8 min)
================================================================
1. Go to   https://www.netlify.com   and click "Sign up".
2. Choose "Sign up with GitHub" (easiest — it links the two).
   Approve/authorize when it asks.
3. Once you're in the Netlify dashboard, click
   "Add new site"  ->  "Import an existing project".
4. Click "Deploy with GitHub" (authorize again if asked).
5. In the list of repositories, click "tatum-time".
6. Leave every box on the next screen exactly as it is
   (don't type anything in "build command" or "publish
   directory") and click "Deploy tatum-time".
7. Wait ~30 seconds. You'll get a live web address that looks
   like   https://random-words-123.netlify.app
   Click it — your site is LIVE on the internet. 🎉

(Optional, to get a nicer address)
8. Click "Site configuration" -> "Change site name" and type
   something like  tatumtime  to get
   https://tatumtime.netlify.app


================================================================
  PART 4 — TURN ON LOGINS  (about 6 min)
================================================================
This is what lets Dan (and only Dan) log in to post.

1. In your site's Netlify dashboard, look in the left menu for
   "Identity"  (it may sit under a section like "Integrations"
   or "Access & security"). Open it and click "Enable Identity".
2. Still under Identity, find "Registration preferences" (or
   "Registration") and set it to  "Invite only".
   (This stops strangers from signing up.)
3. Now find "Services" -> "Git Gateway" and click "Enable
   Git Gateway".

That's the technical part finished.


================================================================
  PART 5 — INVITE DAN  (about 2 min)
================================================================
1. Still on the Identity page, click "Invite users".
2. Type Dan's email address and send the invite.
3. Dan gets an email titled something like "You've been invited
   to join...". He clicks the link in it and sets his own
   password.

   IMPORTANT for Dan: when he clicks that email link it opens
   the website. He should click "Accept the invite" / set his
   password right there. If the login box ever doesn't pop up,
   he can add  /admin  to the web address (see Part 6).


================================================================
  PART 6 — HOW DAN WRITES A POST (send him THIS part)
================================================================
1. Go to the site's admin page. It's the website address with
   /admin on the end, for example:
        https://tatumtime.netlify.app/admin
2. Log in with the email + password from the invite.
3. Click  "Blog Posts"  ->  "All Posts".
4. Click  "Add Posts"  to start a new one, and fill in:
      • Headline      — the title
      • Banner Emoji  — one emoji (e.g. 🏀)
      • Category      — pick from the menu
      • Date          — type it how you want it shown
      • Game Box-Score Link (optional) — paste an NBA.com box
        score web address and a green "See full game stats"
        button shows up automatically. Leave blank for none.
      • Article — write normally. The toolbar does bold,
        headings and lists. Tip: wrap a stat in == == to make
        it a green tag, like  ==28.3 PPG==
5. Drag your NEW post to the TOP of the list (newest first).
6. Click "Publish" (top of the screen).
7. Wait about a minute, refresh the website — your post is live.

No code. No Claude. Just the form.


================================================================
  PART 7 — OPTIONAL EXTRAS (do these any time, after Part 6)
================================================================
• COMMENTS on blog posts:
  Follow "SETUP-COMMENTS.txt" (about 5 minutes). Until then,
  each post just shows a small "comments not on yet" note.

• AUTO-UPDATING RSS FEED:
  The site already has a feed at  YOURSITE/feed.xml . To make it
  refresh automatically whenever Dan publishes, go to your
  Netlify site -> "Site configuration" -> "Build & deploy" ->
  "Build settings", and set the Build command to:
        node build-rss.js
  (Leave "Publish directory" empty.) That's it. If you skip
  this, the feed still works — it just won't include brand-new
  posts until it's regenerated.
  NOTE: also open build-rss.js once and change the SITE address
  near the top to your real Netlify address.

================================================================
  IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG
================================================================
• "I don't see Identity in Netlify."
  Use the dashboard search box and search "Identity", or look
  under "Integrations" / "Access & security". Netlify moves
  menus around sometimes.

• "Dan's invite link didn't open a login box."
  Go straight to  YOURSITE/admin  and click "Login with
  Netlify Identity", then "Sign up" once with the invited
  email to set the password.

• "I changed the site name and want the admin to match."
  Open  admin/config.yml  on GitHub, update the two "site_url"
  lines to your real address, and commit. (Optional — it
  usually works without this.)
